Explore General Geometry Concepts

This tab previews the major ideas students will see in Geometry and the prerequisite skills behind them. It is for review, not scoring.

Pythagorean Theorem

Used in right triangles and coordinate distance problems.

  • Identify the hypotenuse.
  • Use a² + b² = c².
  • Be ready for square roots.
Example: Legs 6 and 8 give c² = 36 + 64 = 100, so c = 10.

Transformations

Figures can move, flip, turn, or resize on a coordinate plane.

  • Translation: slide
  • Reflection: flip
  • Rotation: turn
  • Dilation: resize
Example: (x, y) → (x + 4, y - 2) moves right 4 and down 2.

Linear Equations & Graphing

Geometry uses lines, slopes, coordinate points, and equations.

  • Slope is change in y over change in x.
  • y = mx + b shows slope and y-intercept.
  • Parallel lines have the same slope.
Example: y = 2x + 3 has slope 2 and y-intercept 3.

Simplifying Radicals

Radicals appear in missing side lengths and exact answers.

  • Break the number into perfect-square factors.
  • √50 = √25 × √2 = 5√2.
Example: √72 = √36 × √2 = 6√2.

Ratios, Fractions, and Scale

Similarity, dilation, scale drawings, and proportions depend on ratios.

  • Use ratios to compare corresponding sides.
  • Convert mixed numbers when needed.
  • Scale factor tells how much a shape changes.
Example: A 3:2 scale factor means every length is multiplied by 3/2.

Logic and Proof Readiness

Geometry asks students to justify conclusions, not just compute answers.

  • Use facts in order.
  • Support claims with reasons.
  • Look for what must be true.
Example: If all squares are rectangles and a figure is a square, it must be a rectangle.
